The Georgia-Pacific paper mill sits directly between the banks of the mighty Columbia River and the heart of downtown Camas. In 1883, newsman Henry Pittock bought land to build a mill for producing the Oregonian newspaper. The town formed soon after.

Some of the original paper machines are still in operation today, and the mill is one of only two Georgia-Pacific sites that produces copy paper. More than 400 current employees also take pride in making paper towels for restaurants, offices and stadiums across the U.S.

/ Getting It Done The Camas Way /

The town’s unofficial motto is a glimpse at the personal pride and ownership that locals put into their work. For the Harts, it’s a way of life.

“It’s important that I carry on my grandpa and my father’s legacy here of getting things done properly and being that dependable person people can look to, because the livelihood of everyone here is based upon our ability to keep things running,” Tony says.
